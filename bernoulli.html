Bernoulli Distribution

The Bernoulli distribution is a discrete probability distribution that models a single experiment with only two possible outcomes: success (1) and failure (0). The probability of success is denoted as \(p\), and the probability of failure is \(1-p\).

Below is an interactive visualization of the Bernoulli distribution. Adjust the probability of success (\(p\)) to see how it affects the probability mass function (PMF).

Example

Consider a Bernoulli distribution with a probability of success (\(p\)) equal to 0.5. The probability mass function (PMF) for this distribution is given by:

PMF: \( P(X = x) = p^x(1 - p)^{1 - x} \), where \( x \in \{0, 1\} \)

Notation:

  • X: Random variable representing the outcome (either 0 or 1).
  • p: Probability of success (1).
  • 1 - p: Probability of failure (0).

Parameters:

  • p: Probability of success (0 ≤ p ≤ 1).

Mean of Bernoulli Distribution:

The mean (\( \mu \)) of a Bernoulli distribution is the probability of success (\( p \)):

Mean: \( \mu = p \)

Variance:

The variance (\( \sigma^2 \)) of a Bernoulli distribution is given by:

Variance: \( \sigma^2 = p(1 - p) \)
