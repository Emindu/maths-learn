about

The Bernoulli distribution models a single experiment with exactly two outcomes — success (X = 1) with probability p and failure (X = 0) with probability 1 − p. It is the simplest non-trivial probability distribution and the building block for nearly every discrete model in statistics.

Variance peaks at p = 0.5, where both outcomes are equally likely and uncertainty is greatest. It falls to zero as p approaches 0 or 1, where the outcome becomes increasingly certain. Entropy follows the same arc: one bit at p = 0.5, zero bits at the extremes.

In Bayesian inference, the Bernoulli likelihood has the Beta distribution as its conjugate prior. Observing n successes and m failures updates Beta(α, β) to Beta(α + n, β + m), making posterior computation exact and closed-form.
