FORMULA

CDF

F(x; a, b) = (x−a)/(b−a) for a ≤ x ≤ b

Quantile (inverse CDF)

Q(p) = a + p·(b−a), p ∈ [0, 1]
ABOUT

The Uniform distribution is the maximum-entropy distribution on a bounded interval — it assigns equal probability density to every point in [a, b]. This makes it the natural model when all outcomes in a range are equally likely and no other information is available. It is defined entirely by its endpoints, and the PDF is simply the constant 1/(b−a).

The distribution is perfectly symmetric, so mean, median, and mode coincide at the midpoint (a+b)/2. Its kurtosis of 9/5 is below 3, meaning it has lighter tails than the Normal — a consequence of the hard cutoff at the boundaries. Skewness is zero due to symmetry.

In practice, the Uniform is ubiquitous as a prior in Bayesian inference (reflecting complete ignorance within a range), and as the foundation of random number generation — virtually every pseudo-random number generator produces Uniform(0,1) samples, from which all other distributions are derived via inverse CDF transforms.
