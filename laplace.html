ABOUT

The Laplace distribution (also called the double exponential) is the continuous analogue of the geometric distribution and resembles the Normal distribution but with heavier, exponential tails. It is defined by its sharp peak at the mode μ and tails that decay as e^(−|x−μ|/b) rather than the faster Gaussian decay.

In statistics, the Laplace distribution is the maximum likelihood model for absolute deviations: it is to L1 regression what the Normal is to L2 regression. In signal processing and machine learning, Laplace priors (via LASSO / L1 regularisation) promote sparsity in parameter estimates — a Laplace prior on coefficients produces exactly the LASSO solution when combined with a least-squares likelihood.
