ABOUT

The Exponential distribution models waiting times between successive events in a Poisson process. If events arrive at average rate λ per unit time, the waiting time until the next event has an Exponential(λ) distribution. Its most distinctive property is memorylessness: given that you have already waited t units, the remaining wait is still Exponential(λ), as if you had just started.

The rate parameter λ governs both shape and scale. Larger λ means more frequent events, so shorter expected waiting times (mean = 1/λ). The distribution is always right-skewed (skewness = 2), with a mode at zero — short waits are always most likely. Among all non-negative distributions with a fixed mean, the Exponential maximises entropy, making it a natural choice when only the average rate is known.

The Exponential is a special case of the Gamma distribution (shape k = 1). Summing k independent Exponential(λ) variables yields a Gamma(k, λ), which gives the Erlang distribution used in queueing theory. The geometric distribution is its discrete analogue.
