FORMULA

PMF

P(X = k) = C(n, k) · p^k · (1−p)^(n−k),   k ∈ {0, 1, ..., n}

CDF

F(k) = Σ_{i=0}^{k} C(n,i) · p^i · (1−p)^(n−i)

MGF

M(t) = (1 − p + p·e^t)^n
ABOUT

The Binomial distribution counts the number of successes in n independent Bernoulli(p) trials. It is the fundamental model for repeated binary experiments — every time you run an experiment with two outcomes a fixed number of times, the count of successes follows a Binomial distribution.

The shape depends on p and n. When p = 0.5 the distribution is symmetric; when p < 0.5 it is right-skewed (more probability mass near 0); when p > 0.5 it is left-skewed. As n grows large, the Central Limit Theorem ensures the distribution converges to a Normal shape regardless of p — a fact exploited in many approximations.

When np and n(1−p) are both large (> 5 is a rule of thumb), the Binomial(n, p) is well approximated by Normal(np, np(1−p)). For rare events with large n and small p, the Poisson(np) approximation is more accurate.
